# The CLEAR Prompt Framework

A practical framework for writing clearer AI prompts, developed from a literature review, personal experimentation, and a survey of more than 80 South African university students conducted in July 2026.

## Why CLEAR?

Many survey participants said they were frustrated when AI responses were too vague, inaccurate, or generic. Common concerns included receiving irrelevant answers, copying AI output without fully understanding it, and struggling to get responses that stayed focused on the task.

The CLEAR framework provides a simple checklist that can help improve prompt quality and produce more relevant responses.

## The Framework

### **C – Context**

Provide relevant background information, such as who you are, what you're working on, and why you need the response.

*Without enough context, the AI has to make assumptions, which can lead to less accurate answers.*

### **L – Limitations**

Set clear boundaries for the response. For example:

* Do not use sources newer than 2024.
* Only use the information provided.
* Do not speculate.

Clear limitations help keep responses focused and reduce unsupported assumptions.

### **E – Examples**

Show the format, structure, or style you want. Even a single example can make your expectations much clearer than a long list of instructions.

### **A – Audience**

Specify who the response is intended for. An explanation written for a 14-year-old will differ from one written for a university lecturer.

### **R – Requirements**

State any specific constraints, such as the desired length, tone, content to include, or information to avoid.

## Example Progression

### Basic prompt

> "Explain recursion."

This prompt provides little context, so the response could vary from a simple definition to a detailed computer science explanation.

### Improved prompt

> "Explain recursion to a 14-year-old who understands loops but has never seen recursion. Use one analogy, one Python example, explain one common misconception, and end with a practice question."

This prompt includes:

* **Context:** The learner understands loops.
* **Limitations:** Keep the explanation suitable for a beginner.
* **Examples:** Use an analogy and a Python example.
* **Audience:** A 14-year-old learner.
* **Requirements:** Include a misconception and finish with a practice question.

## Survey Connection

Responses from the EMZAMS AI survey reflected a common pattern:

* Students found AI more useful when prompts were specific.
* AI was commonly used to summarise readings and explain concepts.
* Some participants felt AI responses were too generic or unfocused.

The CLEAR framework was created to address these common challenges by encouraging users to write prompts that are more specific, structured, and easier for AI systems to interpret.
