# AI Limitations and Common Failure Modes

This section is based on findings from the literature, hands-on experimentation, and a survey of more than 80 South African university students conducted in July 2026.

## 1. Hallucination (Fabricated Information)

AI models can generate information that appears accurate but is incorrect or unsupported. This may include invented facts, citations, statistics, or references. Several survey participants noted that AI responses should be checked rather than accepted without verification.

**Why it happens:** Large language models generate text by predicting likely sequences of words rather than verifying information against a source of truth. As a result, they can sometimes produce convincing but inaccurate content.

**Survey example:** "AI can produce slop sometimes." — Brian Albertus

## 2. Over-reliance and Academic Integrity

Survey participants identified copying AI-generated work without understanding or acknowledging it as one of the most common forms of inappropriate AI use. Relying too heavily on AI can reduce opportunities for critical thinking and independent learning.

**Common survey responses included:**

* Submitting AI-generated work as your own.
* Using AI to write entire essays or assignments.
* Not acknowledging when AI was used.

## 3. Ambiguous or Unclear Prompts

When prompts are vague or lack sufficient detail, AI may produce responses that are too broad or do not match the user's intended goal.

**Survey connection:** Some participants described AI responses as "too generic," which can often result from prompts that provide limited context or unclear instructions.

## 4. False Assumptions and Conflicting Instructions

If a prompt contains incorrect assumptions or conflicting requirements, the AI may attempt to satisfy both instead of identifying the inconsistency.

**Example:** "Summarise this 100-page document in 50 words while keeping every important detail."

In situations like this, the requested outcome is difficult or impossible to achieve fully, so the response may not meet expectations.

## 5. Overconfidence and Limited Expression of Uncertainty

AI models often present answers in a confident tone, even when the information is incomplete or incorrect. For this reason, important facts, sources, and calculations should be verified independently.

**Survey connection:** Several students mentioned the need to fact-check AI responses because the model can generate incorrect information or unsupported references.

## 6. Bias and Ethical Considerations

AI systems can reflect biases that exist in their training data and may not always recognise ethical concerns or provide balanced perspectives. Human judgement remains important, particularly when using AI for academic, professional, or sensitive tasks.

**Survey connection:** Some participants commented that AI should support learning rather than replace independent thinking.

## Why This Matters

Understanding these common limitations can help users interact with AI more effectively. Recognising where AI performs well—and where it may make mistakes—makes it easier to write clearer prompts, evaluate responses critically, and use AI as a support tool rather than a replacement for human judgement.
