# Module 1: AI Communication Basics

## Objective

Understand the characteristics of an effective AI prompt and explore how the level of detail in a prompt can influence the quality of the response.

## Opening Activity

Students read anonymised comments from the survey describing their experiences with AI, for example:

* "AI gives slop sometimes."
* "It's not always accurate."
* "I don't know how to ask it to do things properly."

**Discussion question:** What do these comments have in common? What challenges do they highlight when interacting with AI?

## Lesson

1. Explain that AI responds based on the information and instructions it is given, so unclear prompts can lead to less useful responses.
2. Introduce the **CLEAR** framework:

   * **C**ontext
   * **L**imitations
   * **E**xamples
   * **A**udience
   * **R**equirements
3. Compare a basic prompt with a revised prompt that applies the CLEAR framework, and discuss how the responses differ.

## Exercise

Students are given a basic prompt and asked to improve it using the CLEAR framework. They then evaluate the revised prompt using the Prompt Evaluation Rubric.

## Reflection

* How did adding more context affect the AI's response?
* Which part of the CLEAR framework made the biggest difference?
* What would you change if you revised your prompt again?

## Survey Connection

Responses from the EMZAMS AI survey indicated that many students use AI regularly for learning and academic tasks. Some participants also described difficulties in writing effective prompts or receiving responses that were too broad or not closely aligned with what they wanted. This module uses those observations as a starting point for developing practical prompting skills.
