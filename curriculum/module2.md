# Module 2: Avoiding Common Pitfalls

## Objective

Develop the ability to recognise common limitations of AI-generated responses and explore how prompt design can help reduce or identify these issues.

## Opening Activity

Present three AI-generated responses to the same prompt (selected from the experiments folder) and ask:

> **Which response would you trust most, and why?**

Encourage students to discuss what influenced their decision. Consider factors such as accuracy, supporting evidence, clarity, completeness, and the confidence with which the response is presented.

## Lesson

Introduce the common AI limitations discussed in the **AI Limitations and Common Failure Modes** section.

1. **Hallucination** – The model generates information, references, or facts that are incorrect or unsupported.
2. **Over-reliance** – Users accept or submit AI-generated work without sufficient understanding or review.
3. **Ambiguity** – The prompt lacks sufficient detail, leading to responses that may be too broad or misaligned with the user's intention.
4. **False Premises** – The prompt contains an incorrect assumption, and the AI responds based on that assumption rather than challenging it.
5. **Overconfidence** – The AI presents information confidently even when it may be inaccurate or uncertain.

For each limitation, examine an example drawn from the experiment collection or the survey findings. Discuss how revising the prompt—or critically reviewing the response—could help reduce the likelihood of these issues affecting the final result.

**Key principle:** AI responses are influenced by both the quality of the prompt and the capabilities and limitations of the model. While clearer prompts can often improve the relevance and focus of responses, they cannot eliminate every type of error. Critical evaluation remains an important part of using AI effectively.

## Exercise

Students are given the following prompt:

> "Write a 300-word summary of the causes of World War I."

They should:

1. Identify which AI limitations or failure modes might affect the response.
2. Rewrite the prompt to reduce at least two of these risks.
3. Run both versions using the same AI tool and compare the responses.
4. Reflect on whether the revised prompt addressed the identified issues.

## Reflection

* Which AI limitation did you find most surprising?
* What factors would you consider before trusting an AI-generated response?
* What changes would you make to the way you write prompts after completing this activity?

## Survey Connection

The EMZAMS AI survey found that many participants identified copying AI-generated work without sufficient understanding as a common concern. This module encourages students to engage more critically with AI by developing prompting skills, evaluating responses, and using AI as a learning support tool rather than relying solely on the generated output.
