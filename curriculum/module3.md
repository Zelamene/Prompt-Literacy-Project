# Module 3: Critical Prompting & Responsible AI Use

## Objective

Develop skills for evaluating AI-generated content, verifying information, refining prompts through iteration, and using AI in ways that support academic integrity.

## Opening Activity

Provide students with an AI-generated response that contains a subtle factual error and a missing or incorrect citation.

Ask:

> **How would you verify this information? What changes would you make before using it in an assignment?**

Encourage students to explain the steps they would take rather than simply identifying the error.

## Lesson

### 1. Fact-checking workflows

Discuss practical strategies for verifying AI-generated information.

* Ask the AI to provide sources or references, then verify that they exist and support the claims made.
* Compare the response with information from reliable sources, such as academic databases, official publications, or reputable search results.
* Ask the AI to review its own response by identifying areas of uncertainty, assumptions it made, or claims that should be independently verified.

Emphasise that AI-generated references and citations should always be checked before they are used in academic work.

### 2. Ethical use of AI

Discuss appropriate and inappropriate uses of AI in academic settings.

Consider questions such as:

* When does AI support learning?
* When might AI reduce opportunities for learning or conflict with academic integrity policies?
* How do time pressure, workload, or uncertainty influence students' decisions about using AI?

The EMZAMS AI survey identified time pressure and copying AI-generated work as common concerns. Prompt-writing skills may help students use AI more effectively, but they should be combined with critical thinking and independent evaluation.

### 3. Iterative prompting

Introduce AI interactions as an iterative process rather than a single prompt and response.

For example:

> "Explain X."

↓

> "Add a practical example."

↓

> "Review your previous answer and identify any information that may need verification."

↓

> "Rewrite the explanation for a non-technical audience."

This process encourages students to refine and evaluate AI-generated responses instead of accepting the first answer without question.

### 4. Academic Integrity and Disclosure

Discuss institutional expectations regarding AI use.

Topics may include:

* When AI use should be acknowledged.
* How different universities approach disclosure.
* Why it is important to follow the academic integrity policies of a particular institution or course.

Where appropriate, provide examples of acceptable disclosure statements based on institutional guidance.

## Exercise

Students are given the following question:

> **How does inflation affect consumer spending in emerging markets?**

They should:

1. Write an initial prompt and save the AI-generated response.
2. Select two factual claims from the response and verify them using reliable sources.
3. Write two follow-up prompts that improve, clarify, or verify the original response.
4. Write a short reflection addressing the question:

> **What role did you play, and what role did the AI play, in producing the final answer?**

## Reflection

* How confident are you in the accuracy of the final response, and why?
* What steps helped you evaluate the quality of the AI-generated information?
* Where would you draw the boundary between using AI as a learning tool and relying on it too heavily?
* How would you explain your use of AI to a lecturer or supervisor?

## Survey Connection

The EMZAMS AI survey identified concerns about students relying on AI-generated work without sufficient understanding. This module encourages a more deliberate approach by emphasising prompt refinement, fact-checking, critical evaluation, and responsible AI use. The aim is to help students use AI as a learning support tool while remaining actively involved in evaluating and improving the final output.
