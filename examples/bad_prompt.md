# Example: Bad Prompt

## Prompt
What is machine learning?

## AI Output (from Google Gemini Flash)

> At its simplest, machine learning (ML) is a way of teaching computers to make decisions or predictions by showing them data, rather than writing a strict set of rules for them to follow.
>
> Think of traditional programming like a recipe: you give the computer the exact ingredients (data) and instructions (code), and it produces a cake. With machine learning, you show the computer 1,000 pictures of cakes and 1,000 pictures of pies, and it figures out the "recipe" to tell them apart on its own.
>
> *(Output continues with types of machine learning, examples, and applications—approximately 300 words in total.)*

## Analysis

### Why is this prompt less effective?

* **No context.** The prompt provides little information about who is asking the question or why they need the explanation. As a result, the AI generates a broad overview that may be appropriate for a general audience but is not tailored to a specific learning objective.

* **No audience specified.** The response introduces terms such as *supervised learning* and *neural networks* without knowing the reader's prior knowledge. For some learners this level of detail may be suitable, while others may benefit from additional explanation or simpler language.

* **No format or constraints.** The prompt does not specify the desired length, structure, or level of detail. The AI therefore determines these aspects itself, which can result in responses that vary in organisation and depth.

* **No boundaries.** The response includes examples, applications, and different types of machine learning even though these were not explicitly requested. While this additional information may be useful, it may also include details that are beyond what the user intended to learn.

### What this example demonstrates

This example illustrates how a broad prompt gives the AI considerable flexibility in deciding what information to include and how to present it. For users seeking a general introduction, this may be sufficient. However, when the goal is to support a specific learning task, adding context, identifying the intended audience, and specifying the desired structure can help produce a more focused response.

Within the CLEAR framework, this prompt is missing several key elements:

* **Context:** Why is the explanation needed?
* **Audience:** Who is the explanation intended for?
* **Requirements:** What should the response include, and how should it be organised?

Including these elements provides clearer guidance for the AI and generally leads to responses that are better aligned with the user's needs.

### Survey Connection

Participants in the EMZAMS AI survey reported that AI responses were sometimes too broad or did not address what they were trying to learn. This example demonstrates how adding context, audience information, and clear requirements can help guide the AI toward a more focused and relevant response.
