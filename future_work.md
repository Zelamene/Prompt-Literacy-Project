# Future Work and Open Questions

The Prompt Literacy Project is intended to evolve as additional research, feedback, and practical experience become available. The following areas represent possible directions for future development and investigation.

## Potential Next Steps

1. **Evaluate the CLEAR framework across multiple AI models**
   Compare the same prompts using models such as ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and selected open-source models to explore whether the framework performs consistently across different systems.

2. **Develop a prompt exercise library**
   Create a collection of annotated prompt-writing exercises that educators and students can use for teaching, practice, and self-assessment.

3. **Expand the survey**
   Collect responses from a broader range of universities, academic disciplines, and year levels to strengthen the evidence base and identify patterns across different student populations.

## Research Questions

### Generalisability

How well does the CLEAR framework support different types of tasks, such as creative writing, technical problem-solving, and analytical reasoning? Further evaluation could determine whether a single framework is appropriate across domains or whether task-specific adaptations are beneficial.

### Multilingual Prompting

How does prompt quality vary when prompts are written in languages such as isiXhosa or isiZulu, or when users switch between languages within the same prompt? Additional research could examine how effectively current AI models support multilingual prompting in South African contexts.

### Long-Term Skill Development

Do students continue to apply effective prompting techniques after completing prompt literacy training, or do their prompting habits change over time? Longitudinal studies could help answer this question.

### Prompt Robustness

How sensitive are AI models to small changes in prompt wording? Investigating prompt robustness may help identify common sources of misunderstanding and inform strategies for writing more reliable prompts.

### Model Differences

Do different AI models respond consistently to the same prompt, or are there systematic differences in interpretation and output? Understanding these differences may help users adapt prompting strategies to different systems.
