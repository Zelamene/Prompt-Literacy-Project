# Experiment: Instruction Following

## Objective

Explore how increasing the number and specificity of instructions influences an AI model's ability to produce responses that match the requested format, structure, and content.

## Prompt Progression

### Initial Prompt

> Write a short essay about climate change.

### Revised Prompt

> Write a 500-word essay on climate change that includes an introduction, three body paragraphs, and a conclusion. Use simple language.

### Improved Prompt

> Write a 450–500 word persuasive essay arguing that South Africa should increase its investment in renewable energy.
>
> Structure:
>
> * Introduction (hook and thesis statement)
> * Three body paragraphs covering economic, environmental, and social justice arguments
> * Conclusion
>
> Use language appropriate for a first-year university student. Include one statistic with a source in each body paragraph. Do not use bullet points. Do not exceed 520 words.

### Final Prompt

> You are writing for *The Conversation Africa*. Write a 480–500 word opinion article arguing that South Africa should increase investment in renewable energy.
>
> Follow these requirements:
>
> * Title: Maximum of eight words.
> * Introduction: Include one realistic fictional anecdote and a clear thesis statement.
> * Body Paragraph 1: Economic argument supported by one statistic from 2024 with a citation.
> * Body Paragraph 2: Environmental argument supported by one statistic from 2024 with a citation.
> * Body Paragraph 3: Social justice argument supported by one statistic from 2024 with a citation.
> * Conclusion: End with a clear call to action.
> * Tone: Persuasive and respectful.
> * Format: Plain text only, without Markdown.
> * Length: Between 480 and 500 words.
>
> After the article, include a brief **Adherence Check** that reports whether the requested structure, word count, and required statistics have been included.

## Analysis

The initial prompt provides only a topic, allowing considerable flexibility in how the AI responds. Each revised version introduces additional guidance by specifying:

* the purpose and type of writing;
* the intended audience;
* the required structure;
* the desired tone and length;
* supporting evidence to include; and
* formatting requirements.

The final prompt also asks the AI to review its own response against the stated requirements. While this self-check may help identify omissions, it should not be treated as confirmation that every requirement has been met. Independent review is still recommended, particularly for factual information, citations, and word counts.

## Key Findings

This experiment illustrates that detailed instructions can help produce responses that are more closely aligned with a user's expectations. However, AI models may still overlook or misinterpret some requirements, especially when prompts contain many simultaneous constraints. Reviewing the output remains an important part of the process.

## Takeaway

When a task has multiple requirements, state them clearly and organise them in a structured format. After the response is generated, verify that the requested content, structure, and formatting have been followed rather than assuming they were applied correctly.

## Survey Connection

Several participants in the EMZAMS AI survey reported using AI to support assignment writing and other structured academic tasks. This experiment demonstrates how clearly defined instructions can improve the consistency of AI-generated responses while highlighting the importance of checking the final output.
